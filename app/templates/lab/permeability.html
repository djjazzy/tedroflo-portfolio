What You're Seeing

The Physics

  • ∇·B = 0 — magnetic flux lines never start or stop; they form closed loops
  • At the material boundary: normal B is continuous, tangential H is continuous
  • High μᵣ → material acts as a low-reluctance path, concentrating flux through itself
  • The exact field solution used here is B = μ₀H₀[1 + kR²(x²−y²)/r⁴] outside

Why It Matters

  • 303/304 SS and titanium are nearly indistinguishable from air magnetically — critical for sensor housings and MRI-adjacent components
  • 17-4 PH dramatically concentrates flux — the same alloy family as 304 SS but ferromagnetic due to its martensitic microstructure
  • In magnetic circuit design, high-μᵣ materials route flux the way copper routes current — this is the basis of transformer cores and motor laminations
